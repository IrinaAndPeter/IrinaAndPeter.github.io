How's the weather in Banff National Park?

August is the warmest month in Banff National Park, with daily temperatures between 20°C and 25°C. There are typically 7-8 wet days in August, so chances for sun are good. Don't forget to grab something warmer for the evenings, though, as temperatures will fall once the sun sets.

So, wait, what's the location, Bow Lake or Peyto Lake?

This will be determined once we get to Banff. How do I get to the lakes?

The lakes are about an hour and 15-30 min drive from Canmore (Bow Lake route, Peyto Lake route). On the day we will have to factor in extra time to account for unexpected traffic.

Will there be walking involved? Can I do it formal shoes?

For both places there will be a short amount of walking (10-20 min) from the parking lot to a suitable spot for the ceremony. For Bow Lake, that will likely mean walking over pebbles by the lake shore, while for Peyto Lake that would involve a ~15 min steep ascent. It's recommended to wear flat shoes, or wear walking shoes and change into more formal shoes once on the spot for the ceremony and photos.

What if the weather doesn't cooperate?

Since it is an outdoor ceremony, the weather will be important. In the (hopefully unlikely) case that our rituals for sunshine don't work, we will make every effort to avoid unpleasant weather during the ceremony. That includes small delays to the ceremony or changing location if "that-weather-that-shall-not-be-named" is localised to an area. Do make sure you have an umbrella packed in the worst case scenario.

Is there a fee for Banff National Park?

Yes, all visitors must have a valid park pass for all the days of stay. Passes can be bought in advance from the Banff official website or on arrival at the park gates, or at the Visitor Centres in Banff and Lake Louise. Prices are:
- adult (<65): 10.5 CAD (1 day) or 72.25 CAD (7 days or more)
- adult (>=65): 9 CAD (1 day) or 61.75 CAD (7 days or more)
- children: free

Are there rules for ceremonies in the park?

Yes, the full list of rules is here, but a quick summary includes: max group size of 20 people, no loud music, no drones, no throwing of confetti (including rice, petals etc.), leave the spot as you found it, no open alcohol.

What else is there to do in Banff?

Banff National Park is famous for its vibrant lakes, majestic mountains and easy access to outdoor adventures of all kinds.
The park spans 6,641 square kilometres (2,564 square miles) of striking mountainous terrain in the vast wilderness of the Canadian Rockies. Canada’s first national park and the world’s third, it has a rich heritage as one of the world’s most awe-inspiring mountain destinations.
For more details and inspiration, have a look on the Banff official website.
